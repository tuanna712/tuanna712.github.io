Seismic Stratigraphy Analysis for unstructured trap determining at Block 09-1,Cuu Long Basin (2021)

An environmental, stratigraphic model based on automatic seismic interpretation which reveals multiple potential unstructured oil and gas traps in Cuu Long basin, offshore Vietnam.

Abstract

With the advancement of machine learning, new approaches like automatic and semi-automatic seismic horizon interpretation enable detailed mapping of subsurfaces, even capturing individual seismic events. This project covers a 973 km² 3D4C seismic volume and over 500 wells.

The final results offer a comprehensive perspective on both interpretation and environmental models, revealing potential approaches for identifying unstructured traps in Miocene sandstone. The project also provides a detailed stratigraphic model for the Cuu Long basin, offshore Vietnam.

Key Results

  • Velocity model verified using 82 well ties
  • Model includes over 200 interpreted horizons
  • Environmental model for 973 km² area reveals potential unstructured oil traps

Main Tasks

  • Data collection and well-tie processing
  • Horizon interpretation
  • Velocity modeling
  • Horizon time-depth conversion
  • Scanning and detecting potential traps

Platforms/ Frameworks

  • Seismic Interpretation: Eliis Paleoscan
  • Well-tie and modeling: SLB Petrel
