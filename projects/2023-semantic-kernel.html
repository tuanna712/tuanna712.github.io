Semantic Kernel Training (2023)

A 2-day training workshop for VPI developers focused on exploring chatbot reasoning systems, actions, and planning with private data, using the Semantic Kernel framework.

Abstract

In the context of LLMs increasingly demonstrating exceptional capabilities in text generation tasks, the demand for leveraging LLMs to enhance productivity has become evident. However, the limitations of LLMs lie in their restricted training data and the lack of access to proprietary data from businesses and users. Moreover, the need to train and guide LLMs to execute processes and make decisions on behalf of users is a practical requirement.

The Semantic Kernel (SK) framework , developed by Microsoft Research and offered as a free framework, optimizes prompt engineering with a robust system that enables the development of LLM application solutions integrated with user systems and data. The operation of SK resembles the core functionalities of AI Assistants such as Microsoft Copilot.

To provide knowledge and skills for working with this framework, a training workshop was organized for developers at the Vietnam Petroleum Institute (VPI). Participants were introduced to solutions for building LLM-based application systems using the Semantic Kernel framework. The workshop was hosted by Data Analysis Department (VPI) and the keynote speaker was Mr. Tuan Nguyen.

The slides and primary language used during the workshop were Vietnamese.

Objectives

  • Introduction to the Semantic Kernel Framework
  • Understanding How Semantic Kernel Works
  • Exploring Key Features: Kernel, Functions, Skills, Plugins, Planner, Memory, and Prompt Engineering
  • Hands-On Practice: Building an SK Solution
  • Final Team Competition

Key Results

All participants scored 90% or higher on the final test. Teams performed well in the competition, delivering strong solutions to real-world problems with SK.
