# Seismic Stratigraphy Analysis for Unstructured Trap Determining  
**Block 09-1, Cuu Long Basin (2021)**

An environmental, stratigraphic model based on automatic seismic interpretation that reveals multiple potential unstructured oil and gas traps in the Cuu Long Basin, offshore Vietnam.

---

### Abstract

With the advancement of machine learning, new approaches such as automatic and semi-automatic seismic horizon interpretation enable detailed mapping of subsurfaces, including individual seismic events. This project covers a **973 km² 3D4C seismic volume** and **over 500 wells**.

The final results provide a comprehensive perspective on both interpretation and environmental models, revealing effective approaches for identifying unstructured traps in **Miocene sandstone**. In addition, the project delivers a detailed stratigraphic model for the Cuu Long Basin, offshore Vietnam.

---

### Key Results

The velocity model was validated through 82 well ties, and the interpretation includes more than 200 seismic horizons. Together, these results enabled the construction of an environmental model covering 973 km², which highlights multiple potential unstructured oil and gas traps.

---

### Main Tasks

The work involved collecting seismic and well data, performing well-tie processing, interpreting seismic horizons, building velocity models, converting horizons from time to depth, and scanning the subsurface to detect potential hydrocarbon traps.

---

### Platforms / Frameworks

Seismic interpretation was carried out using Eliis Paleoscan, while well-tie analysis and geological modeling were performed in SLB Petrel.
